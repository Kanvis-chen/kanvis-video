# Scene Grammar

Choose visuals from meaning, not paragraph order.

| Meaning | Graphic type | Preferred avatar layout |
|---|---|---|
| Hook or thesis | hero claim | center-medium or left-medium |
| Three to five points | step/list cards | right picture-in-picture |
| A versus B | split comparison | small corner picture-in-picture |
| Process | flow or timeline | side presenter |
| Quantitative claim | number card or chart | avatar absent or small |
| Evidence or interface | screenshot/document focus | corner picture-in-picture |
| Hierarchy | tree or layered stack | side presenter |
| Quotation | full-screen quote | avatar absent |
| Summary | recap cards | center-small or side presenter |

## Rhythm rules

- Establish the human presenter during the opening.
- Change composition when the information role changes, not on arbitrary intervals.
- Use at least three visual roles in videos longer than 60 seconds.
- Keep on-screen text shorter than the narration.
- Let evidence, charts, and interfaces occupy the main canvas.
- Use full-screen avatar sparingly; the article's information remains the main picture.

## Safe composition

Reserve a caption band and platform UI margins. Do not place essential text behind the avatar. Prefer a stable lower-right or lower-left presenter zone per scene, and switch sides only for a reason.
