# Editorial Rules

## Source fidelity

- Save the supplied article unchanged.
- Separate direct source claims from inferred transitions.
- Keep names, numbers, qualifications, and causal direction accurate.
- Do not add external facts unless research was authorized and sources are recorded.

## Spoken adaptation

- Open with the audience's problem or a counterintuitive conclusion.
- Use short sentences and audible transitions.
- Convert dense lists into grouped beats.
- Remove repeated setup, promotional padding, and written-language filler.
- End with a useful conclusion or action, not a generic slogan.

Aim for roughly 220–280 Chinese characters per minute after pauses. Treat this as a calibration range, not a hard limit.

## Pronunciation

Extract English terms, acronyms, uncommon names, version numbers, URLs, and ambiguous Chinese polyphones. Record normalized spoken forms before voice generation.

## Safety

Flag medical, legal, financial, political, or rapidly changing claims for verification. Never simulate endorsement by a real person without explicit authorization.
